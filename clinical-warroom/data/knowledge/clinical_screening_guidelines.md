# Clinical Gait Screening Guidelines

## Purpose

This document provides standardized guidelines for gait screening in clinical settings, with focus on identifying patients at risk for falls and mobility impairment.

## Indications for Gait Screening

### Universal Screening
All patients aged ≥65 years should receive annual gait screening.

### Targeted Screening
Screen patients of any age with:
- History of falls (past 12 months)
- Neurological conditions (Parkinson's, stroke, MS)
- Lower extremity joint conditions
- Diabetes with peripheral neuropathy
- Balance complaints
- Fear of falling
- Recent hospitalization
- Polypharmacy (≥4 medications)

## Screening Components

### 1. Gait Speed (Preferred Metric)

**Protocol:**
- Measure time to walk 4 or 10 meters
- Use standing start
- Patient walks at comfortable pace
- Calculate: Distance / Time = Speed (m/s)

**Interpretation:**
| Speed (m/s) | Classification |
|-------------|----------------|
| > 1.0       | Normal |
| 0.8 - 1.0   | Intermediate |
| < 0.8       | Abnormal |

### 2. Timed Up and Go (TUG) Test

**Protocol:**
1. Patient sits in standard chair (46 cm seat height)
2. On "Go," patient stands, walks 3 meters, turns, returns, sits
3. Record time in seconds

**Interpretation:**
| Time (seconds) | Classification |
|----------------|----------------|
| < 10           | Normal mobility |
| 10 - 14        | Mild impairment |
| 15 - 20        | Moderate impairment |
| > 20           | Severe impairment, high fall risk |

### 3. Single-Leg Stance Test

**Protocol:**
- Patient stands on one leg, eyes open
- Time until foot touches down or arms used for balance
- Test both sides

**Interpretation:**
| Duration | Age 60-69 Normal | Age 70-79 Normal | Age 80+ Normal |
|----------|------------------|------------------|----------------|
| Seconds  | > 25 | > 15 | > 5 |

### 4. Instrumented Gait Analysis

When available, computerized gait analysis provides:
- Cadence (steps/min)
- Stride length (m)
- Gait speed (m/s)
- Asymmetry index
- Step variability (CV%)

## Red Flags Requiring Immediate Evaluation

1. **Acute gait change** - New onset or sudden worsening
2. **Focal neurological signs** - Weakness, numbness, visual changes
3. **Recent fall with injury**
4. **Gait speed decline > 0.1 m/s** over 1 year
5. **New asymmetry** - Previously symmetric gait becomes asymmetric

## Referral Criteria

### Physical Therapy Referral
- Gait speed < 1.0 m/s
- TUG > 12 seconds
- Asymmetry index > 0.10
- History of falls

### Neurology Referral
- New asymmetric gait
- Festination or freezing
- Parkinsonian features
- Progressive unexplained decline

### Orthopedic Referral
- Pain-related gait abnormality
- Joint deformity affecting gait
- Leg length discrepancy > 2 cm

## Documentation Requirements

Each gait screening should document:
1. Screening method used
2. Quantitative result (time, speed, etc.)
3. Classification (normal/abnormal)
4. Comparison to previous screenings
5. Referrals made
6. Follow-up plan

## Follow-up Intervals

| Risk Level | Screening Interval |
|------------|-------------------|
| Low risk   | Annual |
| Moderate   | Every 6 months |
| High       | Every 3 months |
| Very High  | Monthly |

## Quality Measures

### Screening Rate Target
- ≥90% of eligible patients screened annually

### Documentation Compliance
- ≥95% of screenings with complete documentation

### Intervention Rate
- ≥80% of abnormal screenings with documented intervention plan
