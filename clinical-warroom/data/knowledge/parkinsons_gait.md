# Parkinson's Disease and Gait Characteristics

## Overview

Parkinson's disease (PD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that significantly affects motor function, particularly gait. Understanding the characteristic gait patterns is essential for early detection and monitoring of disease progression.

## Characteristic Gait Features in Parkinson's Disease

### Reduced Stride Length
- PD patients typically exhibit shortened stride length
- Normal stride length: 55-85% of height
- PD patients often show stride length < 45% of height
- Stride length typically decreases as disease progresses

### Decreased Gait Speed
- Normal gait speed: 1.0-1.4 m/s for healthy adults
- PD patients often show speeds < 0.8 m/s
- Speeds < 0.6 m/s indicate significant impairment
- Gait speed is a strong predictor of fall risk

### Cadence Changes
- Normal cadence: 90-120 steps per minute
- PD patients may show either:
  - Reduced cadence (< 80 steps/min) in early stages
  - Increased cadence with shortened steps (festination) in advanced stages

### Festination
- Involuntary shortening and quickening of steps
- Progressive acceleration while walking
- Often precedes freezing episodes
- Associated with forward-leaning posture

### Freezing of Gait (FOG)
- Sudden, brief episodes of inability to initiate or continue walking
- Often triggered by:
  - Turning
  - Approaching doorways or narrow passages
  - Starting to walk
  - Stress or dual tasking
- Strong predictor of falls

### Postural Instability
- Impaired ability to maintain balance
- Reduced postural reflexes
- Forward-leaning posture (stooped)
- Difficulty recovering from perturbations

### Arm Swing Asymmetry
- Reduced arm swing on affected side
- Asymmetry index > 0.15 suggests unilateral involvement
- Can be early sign of PD

## Gait Variability in Parkinson's Disease

### Step-to-Step Variability
- Normal variability (CV): < 5%
- PD patients often show CV > 8%
- Higher variability correlates with fall risk
- Coefficient of variation (CV) of stride time is particularly sensitive

### Stride Time Variability
- Normal stride time variability: < 3%
- PD patients: often > 5%
- Increases with disease severity

## Clinical Implications

### Fall Risk Assessment
Patients with PD are at significantly increased fall risk when:
- Gait speed < 0.7 m/s
- Stride variability CV > 10%
- History of freezing episodes
- Postural instability present

### Disease Stage Correlation
- Hoehn and Yahr Stage 1-2: Mild gait changes, unilateral symptoms
- Hoehn and Yahr Stage 3: Bilateral involvement, postural instability emerges
- Hoehn and Yahr Stage 4-5: Severe gait impairment, assistance required
