# Fall Risk Assessment Guidelines

## Overview

Fall prevention is a critical component of care for elderly and mobility-impaired patients. This document outlines evidence-based indicators and thresholds for fall risk assessment based on gait analysis.

## Primary Fall Risk Indicators

### 1. Gait Speed

Gait speed is the single most predictive indicator of fall risk in older adults.

| Speed (m/s) | Risk Level | Interpretation |
|-------------|------------|----------------|
| > 1.0       | Low        | Independent community ambulation |
| 0.8 - 1.0   | Moderate   | Limited community ambulation |
| 0.6 - 0.8   | High       | Household ambulation only |
| < 0.6       | Very High  | Severe mobility impairment |

**Clinical threshold**: Gait speed < 0.8 m/s indicates elevated fall risk.

### 2. Stride Variability

Step-to-step variability reflects motor control and is strongly associated with fall risk.

| CV (%) | Risk Level | Interpretation |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| < 3    | Low        | Normal variability |
| 3 - 5  | Moderate   | Mildly elevated |
| 5 - 8  | High       | Significant concern |
| > 8    | Very High  | Strong predictor of falls |

**Clinical threshold**: Coefficient of variation > 5% indicates elevated fall risk.

### 3. Gait Asymmetry

Asymmetry between left and right steps indicates unilateral weakness or neurological involvement.

| Asymmetry Index | Risk Level | Interpretation |
|-----------------|------------|----------------|
| < 0.05          | Normal     | Symmetric gait |
| 0.05 - 0.10     | Low        | Minimal asymmetry |
| 0.10 - 0.20     | Moderate   | Notable asymmetry |
| 0.20 - 0.30     | High       | Significant asymmetry |
| > 0.30          | Very High  | Severe asymmetry |

**Clinical threshold**: Asymmetry index > 0.15 warrants investigation.

### 4. Cadence Abnormalities

| Cadence (steps/min) | Interpretation |
|---------------------|----------------|
| 90 - 120            | Normal range |
| 70 - 90             | Below normal, may indicate weakness |
| < 70                | Significantly reduced, mobility impairment |
| > 130               | Festination pattern, Parkinson's concern |

## Secondary Risk Factors

### Medical History
The following conditions increase fall risk:
- Previous falls (strongest predictor)
- Parkinson's disease
- Stroke or CVA
- Diabetes (peripheral neuropathy)
- Vestibular disorders
- Cognitive impairment
- Visual impairment
- Polypharmacy (≥4 medications)

### Medication Effects
Medications that increase fall risk:
- Sedatives and hypnotics
- Antipsychotics
- Antidepressants
- Antihypertensives
- Opioids

## Age-Adjusted Thresholds

Fall risk thresholds should be adjusted for age:

### Ages 60-69
- Gait speed concern: < 1.0 m/s
- Expected decline: 1-2% per year from age 60

### Ages 70-79
- Gait speed concern: < 0.9 m/s
- Increased tolerance for variability (CV < 6%)

### Ages 80+
- Gait speed concern: < 0.8 m/s
- Higher baseline risk regardless of gait parameters
- Multi-factorial assessment essential

## Risk Stratification

### Low Risk
- Gait speed > 1.0 m/s
- Variability CV < 3%
- No falls in past year
- No high-risk medical conditions

### Moderate Risk
- Gait speed 0.8 - 1.0 m/s
- Variability CV 3-5%
- One fall in past year
- One high-risk condition present

### High Risk
- Gait speed < 0.8 m/s
- Variability CV > 5%
- Multiple falls in past year
- Multiple high-risk conditions

### Very High Risk
- Gait speed < 0.6 m/s
- Variability CV > 8%
- Recurrent falls
- Parkinson's or stroke history
- Freezing of gait episodes

## Recommended Interventions by Risk Level

### Low Risk
- Annual reassessment
- General exercise recommendations

### Moderate Risk
- Semi-annual reassessment
- Physical therapy referral
- Home safety evaluation

### High Risk
- Quarterly reassessment
- Immediate physical therapy
- Medication review
- Assistive device consideration
- Home modifications

### Very High Risk
- Monthly monitoring
- Intensive physical therapy
- Comprehensive medication review
- Assistive device prescription
- Supervision during ambulation
